The Story of the Civil War

By Vignesh Karumathil and George John

Welcome! Please choose your story!

You are an African Slave who lives in the South. Despite the North's propaganda of anti-slavery, the South has not given up human labor and continues.

Because of their refusal to quit slavery, the South has seceded from the United States to form their own country, and a war has started because of that.

This could be your chance to escape your master!

Do you want to escape using the Underground Railroad or join the Union Army?

You attempt to escape via the Underground Railroad. Which route do you take?

You successfully reach Canada. You are free! Good ending!

A very popular destination for escaping slaves is Canada. They abolished slavery in 1834, 30 years before the U.S. did.

You took the Pennsylvania route and died. Bad ending!

The Pennsylvania route was very common in having bounty hunters and slave catchers, and slaves being caught were very common.

You join the Union Army. Which battle do you fight?

You survive the Battle of Shiloh and are honored.

The Battle of Shiloh was a very important but less lethal conflict, with a 76% rate of survival, higher than most battles.

You are now a free African American. Do you retire or continue in the military?

You fought at Gettysburg and died. Bad ending!

The Battle of Gettysburg was the most brutal battle in the entirety of the American Civil War, with a death toll of 51 thousand soldiers combined!

You retire and live a normal life. Good ending!

Soldiers could be released at the end of their 3 year service, or the end of the war.

Do you fight at the Overland Campaign or the Battle of the Crater?

You didn't survive the Battle of the Crater. You were surrounded and died by the Confederates!

In the Battle of the Crater, Confederate soldiers surrounded the Union and massacred them. More than 55% Union soldiers died, and you were one of them!

You fought in the Overland Campaign, and nearly died. However, you and your army survived and crippled the Confederacy!

The Overland Campaign was a very deadly campaign with 80-90 thousand deaths combined. On both sides, nearly 50% or more soldiers were killed during this campaign!

Do you fight at Bentonville or Appomattox?

You fought at Bentonville and died. Bad ending!

You fought at Appomattox, ending the war and receiving the best ending!

You are a Confederate soldier, fighting to defend your state. You must choose whether to desert or join campaigns.

You tried to desert the army. You are executed. Bad ending!

You join the campaigns. Which battle do you fight first?

You survive the Battle of Shiloh. Do you retire or fight again?

You fought at Gettysburg and died. Bad ending!

You retire from military service. This is your good ending!

Which battle will you fight now? Choose carefully.

You fought a deadly battle and died. Bad ending!

You fought a less deadly battle. Later, you are captured by the Union army and become a prisoner. This is the only good ending for the Confederate path.
